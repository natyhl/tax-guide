Tax filing, simplified.

A simple guide for international students and student-athletes at the University of San Francisco, especially students receiving taxable scholarships, room and board, or other U.S. income.

Start with the steps Open USF ISSS Taxes

What to know first

  • International students in F, J, M, or Q status generally need to submit U.S. tax forms for each year they were physically present in the U.S.
  • If you had U.S. income, you usually file a tax return.
  • If you had no income, you still usually file Form 8843.
  • USF provides Sprintax access for nonresident tax filing.

International students

For USF students who were physically present in the U.S. in F, J, M, or Q status during the tax year.

Student-athletes

Helpful if you receive scholarship support for room, board, or other living expenses.

Nonresident filers

Most international students who have been in the U.S. for less than five years are likely nonresidents for tax purposes, but Sprintax helps determine this.

Step-by-step

How to file

These are general steps. Always use the official instructions from USF, Sprintax, and the IRS for your final filing.

1

Get an SSN or ITIN, if needed

You need a tax identification number if you are filing a tax return or claiming treaty benefits. Use an SSN if you are eligible through work. Use an ITIN if you are not eligible for an SSN but have taxable income such as taxable scholarship income.

  • USF: Apply for a Social Security Number
  • USF: ITIN instructions
  • IRS: Form W-7 for ITIN
2

Complete Sprintax Calculus

Sprintax Calculus is used by USF to determine tax residency status and possible tax treaty benefits. It is separate from filing your annual tax return.

  • Students working on campus may receive access after completing the Workday onboarding and Form I-9 process.
  • Students receiving taxable scholarships may receive access after the department granting the scholarship provides information to USF Tax Compliance.
  • If you are unsure about timing or access, check with USF or contact Quyen Tu (qtu@usfca.edu).
3

Wait for all tax forms

Before completing your return, make sure you have received all forms that apply to you.

  • W-2 — campus or other employment income.
  • 1042-S — taxable scholarship or treaty-related reporting.
  • 1099-MISC — contractor-type income, if applicable.
  • 592-B — California state tax withholding information.
  • 1095 — health insurance record.
4

Use Sprintax Returns

Access Sprintax through your MyISSS account so you receive the USF discount. Sprintax helps nonresident students prepare federal forms, Form 8843, and state forms if needed.

  • Federal filing access is provided by USF.
  • State tax filing or ITIN support may cost extra.
  • Use Sprintax chat or hello@sprintax.com for tax-preparation questions.
5

Print, sign, and mail

After Sprintax creates your documents, follow the mailing instructions it gives you. Print the forms, sign where required, and mail them to the correct address.

6

Refund or payment

Sprintax will show whether you may receive a refund or whether you need to pay taxes. Follow the payment or refund instructions provided in Sprintax and official tax agency pages.

ITIN

ITIN guide for taxable scholarships

An ITIN is common for students who receive taxable scholarship income but are not eligible for an SSN.

What is an ITIN?

An ITIN is a tax identification number used for tax reporting and filing if you do not have and are not eligible for an SSN.

If you are eligible for an SSN, for example because you work on campus or have practical training authorization, you cannot apply for an ITIN.

Best timing

USF recommends applying for the ITIN separately before tax filing season when possible, preferably by January before you need to file your return. This can help avoid filing late.

Preferred: Apply for an ITIN without a tax return
  1. Create and complete your Sprintax Calculus profile through USF.
  2. Generate Form W-7 in Sprintax Calculus by indicating that you want to apply for an ITIN.
  3. Print and sign Form W-7 with a black pen.
  4. Bring required items to ISSS: passport copy, visa copy, I-94 copy, signed I-20 copy, and original signed W-7.
  5. ISSS mails the application with a letter confirming identity and eligibility.
  6. Watch your mailing address for IRS communication. USF notes this may take about 60–90 days.
  7. After receiving the ITIN, update Sprintax Calculus and use it when filing your tax return.
Alternative: Apply for an ITIN with your tax return

Sprintax Returns can help prepare Form W-7 and your federal tax return together. This route usually requires extra identity verification, such as a certified passport copy from your home government or help from a Certified Acceptance Agent.

When filing an ITIN application with your tax return, you generally file the federal return with the ITIN application first. State filing may need to wait until you receive your tax ID.

Sprintax Calculus

What Sprintax Calculus does

Sprintax Calculus is not the same as filing your taxes. It helps USF determine residency status, withholding, and treaty benefits.

Why USF uses it

The U.S. tax system is pay-as-you-go, meaning tax may be withheld from certain payments. Sprintax Calculus helps determine whether treaty benefits can reduce or eliminate withholding on eligible payments.

Who should complete it

Students with campus jobs or taxable scholarships for room, board, or other living expenses may need to complete a Sprintax Calculus profile. Tuition-only scholarships are generally not taxable.

How access works

Students working on campus are typically invited after Form I-9 onboarding. Students receiving taxable scholarships are invited after the scholarship-granting department provides information to the Office of Tax Compliance and Internal Audit.

Treaty forms

Treaty benefits may require forms such as Form 8233 for campus job compensation or Form W-8BEN for taxable scholarships. USF cannot apply treaty benefits until the correct form includes your SSN or ITIN.

Important

Completing Sprintax Calculus does not mean you filed your annual tax return. You still need to complete the annual filing process through Sprintax Returns or another appropriate tax preparation method.

Deadlines

Key timing

In the Fall (takes 7-11 weeks)

Best time to start ITIN process

If you need an ITIN, USF recommends applying separately before tax filing season when possible.

Check with USF

Submit Sprintax Calculus

The timing is set by USF. If you are unsure, check with USF, your scholarship department, Athletics, or Quyen Tu.

Jan–Mar

Watch for tax forms

W-2 and 1099 forms usually arrive earlier. Forms like 1042-S and 592-B may arrive in March.

Main tax filing deadline

USF lists April 15, 2026 as the individual tax form submission deadline for the 2025 tax year.
